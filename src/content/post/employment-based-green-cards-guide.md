Employment-based green cards give foreign workers permanent residence through their jobs or achievements. They're organized into preference categories — primarily EB-1, EB-2, and EB-3 — each with its own requirements and wait times. Whether you're an employer planning to sponsor or a worker mapping your path, understanding the categories is step one.

## EB-1 — priority workers
The top category, for people with extraordinary ability (EB-1A), outstanding professors and researchers (EB-1B), and multinational managers and executives (EB-1C). EB-1A can be self-petitioned; the others need an employer. No labor certification required.

## EB-2 — advanced degrees and exceptional ability
For roles requiring an advanced degree or workers with exceptional ability. Usually requires PERM labor certification and employer sponsorship — unless you qualify for a National Interest Waiver, which removes both the job offer and PERM.

## EB-3 — skilled and professional workers
For professionals with a bachelor's degree, skilled workers (two-plus years of training/experience), and some other workers. Requires PERM and employer sponsorship. Often has longer waits than EB-2.

## How sponsorship works
For most employer-sponsored categories, the path is PERM, then the I-140 immigrant petition, then adjustment of status or consular processing once a visa is available. Your priority date and country of birth determine the wait via the monthly visa bulletin.
