PERM in 2026 is a multi-stage process, and the total time depends heavily on Department of Labor backlogs, which shift month to month. The honest answer is that a clean PERM case commonly runs well over a year from start to certification, driven mostly by two government queues. Always check the DOL's current processing time disclosures for the latest figures before planning around a date.

## Prevailing wage determination
The PWD is the first wait, and a significant one. Processing times here are published by the DOL and have run several months. Nothing else can start until this comes back.

## Recruitment window
Recruitment itself takes about two to three months by rule. Ads must run for set periods, and there's a mandatory quiet period before filing. This part is predictable because it's defined by regulation.

## PERM application processing
After you file the ETA-9089, the DOL takes additional months to certify, longer if the case is selected for audit. Audit cases add a substantial queue of their own.

## What causes delays
The biggest variables are DOL backlogs (which the agency adjusts based on volume), audit selection, and any errors that trigger a request for more information. Government shutdowns and policy changes can also push timelines. Because these numbers move, treat any single figure as a snapshot and verify the current DOL processing times before committing to a plan.
