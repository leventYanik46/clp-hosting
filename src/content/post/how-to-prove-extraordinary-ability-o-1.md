Meeting the O-1 standard is an evidence exercise. The criteria tell you what categories count; the petition wins or loses on how well you document each one. This is a deep dive into building evidence that USCIS finds persuasive criterion by criterion.

## Awards and recognition
Document the award's significance, not just its existence — who grants it, how selective it is, and what it signals about your standing. A niche award with strong selectivity can carry real weight if you explain it.

## Press and published material
Coverage about you (not by you) in recognized outlets is powerful. Include the full piece, the publication's credibility, and circulation or reach where relevant. Explain why the outlet matters.

## Judging and original contributions
Evidence you've judged others' work (peer review, panels, competitions) and that your own contributions had impact — adoption, citations, results. For contributions, third-party letters explaining the significance are key.

## Expert letters done right
The strongest letters come from independent experts, explain specifically what you did and why it matters, and avoid generic praise. A mix of letters from people who know your work directly and respected figures who can speak to your reputation works best.
