## The E-2's Biggest Limitation

The E-2 treaty investor visa lets you live and work in the U.S. while running your investment enterprise, but it has one significant drawback: there is no direct path from E-2 status to a green card. Unlike the L-1A (which connects to EB-1C) or the H-1B (which connects to PERM and EB-2/EB-3), the E-2 does not have a corresponding immigrant visa category.

This does not mean E-2 holders cannot get green cards. It means the path is indirect, and you need to qualify through a separate category.

## Option 1: EB-5 Immigrant Investor Program

The EB-5 program is the most intuitive fit for E-2 investors because it is also investment-based. You must invest a minimum of $1,050,000 in a new commercial enterprise (or $800,000 if the investment is in a Targeted Employment Area) and create at least 10 full-time jobs for U.S. workers.

**Connection to E-2:** Some E-2 holders can count their existing business toward EB-5 requirements if the investment amount and job creation thresholds are met. However, the EB-5 requires new investment or investment at risk, and restructuring an existing E-2 business to meet EB-5 standards requires careful planning.

**Timeline:** EB-5 processing times vary but typically run 2 to 4 years for the I-526E petition, plus additional time for adjustment of status or consular processing.

## Option 2: EB-1C Multinational Manager or Executive

If you manage a company that has a qualifying relationship with an entity abroad (parent, subsidiary, branch, or affiliate), you may qualify for EB-1C. This requires you to have worked for the foreign entity for at least one year in the three years before your most recent admission to the U.S. in a managerial or executive capacity.

**Connection to E-2:** E-2 investors who own or control businesses both in the U.S. and in their home country sometimes structure the relationship to qualify for EB-1C.

**Timeline:** EB-1 is a priority category, often current for most countries. I-140 processing plus adjustment of status can take 1 to 2 years with premium processing.

## Option 3: EB-2 National Interest Waiver (NIW)

The NIW lets you self-petition for a green card without employer sponsorship or a PERM labor certification. You need to show that your work is in an area of substantial merit and national importance.

**Connection to E-2:** E-2 business owners with a strong track record (job creation, economic impact, innovation) may qualify.

## Option 4: Marriage to a U.S. Citizen

If you are married to a U.S. citizen, your spouse can petition for you as an immediate relative, which has no numerical cap or waiting line.

## Option 5: Employer Sponsorship (PERM + EB-2 or EB-3)

If you work in a qualifying role, an employer can sponsor you through the PERM labor certification process. Self-sponsorship (where you own the company) is possible but scrutinized heavily.

## Maintaining E-2 Status While Pursuing a Green Card

One advantage of the E-2 is that applying for a green card does not automatically disqualify you from E-2 renewals. The E-2 allows "dual intent" in practice. However, consular officers have discretion, and some may view a pending green card application as evidence that you do not intend to maintain E-2 nonimmigrant status. Careful timing and documentation matter.
