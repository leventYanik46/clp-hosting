Yes, and the H-1B is actually built for it. Unlike some visas, the H-1B allows 'dual intent,' meaning you can pursue permanent residence while holding it without jeopardizing your status. For many professionals, the H-1B is the bridge to an employment-based green card.

## Why dual intent matters
Most temporary visas require you to prove you'll leave. Dual intent removes that tension. You can start the green card process, travel, and extend your H-1B even while a green card petition is pending. This is a major practical advantage.

## The typical path
For an employer-sponsored green card, the route is usually PERM labor certification, then the I-140 immigrant petition, then adjustment of status (or consular processing) when a visa number is available. Some workers qualify for EB-1 or EB-2 NIW and skip PERM.

## Timing and priority dates
The wait depends on your category and country of birth, because of per-country limits in the visa bulletin. For some nationalities the wait is short. For others it can be years. The H-1B can usually be extended past its normal limit if a green card process is far enough along.

## Planning the transition
Start early. The PERM and I-140 stages take time, and beginning before your H-1B years run short gives you room to extend. Coordinating the visa and green card timelines is where good planning pays off.
