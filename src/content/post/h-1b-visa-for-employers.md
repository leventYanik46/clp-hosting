Sponsoring an H-1B worker means committing to a defined process and a set of obligations as the employer. The H-1B is the main route for hiring foreign professionals in specialty occupations, but the annual cap and lottery make timing and preparation everything. Here's what sponsorship actually involves.

## What sponsorship requires
You're attesting to pay the required wage, that the role is a specialty occupation needing at least a bachelor's degree in a specific field, and that hiring won't harm similarly employed U.S. workers. You file a Labor Condition Application first, then the petition.

## The annual cap
There's a yearly limit on new H-1Bs, with a set number reserved for advanced-degree holders from U.S. institutions. Once the cap is hit, no more new petitions are accepted that year unless the employer is cap-exempt.

## The registration lottery
Because demand far exceeds the cap, employers electronically register candidates in a set window each spring, and USCIS runs a random selection. Only selected registrations can move forward to a full petition. Registration and selection happen before you file the heavy paperwork.

## Planning around it
Identify candidates early, get wage and role documentation ready before the registration window, and have a backup plan — cap-exempt options, L-1, O-1, or trying again next cycle — for candidates who aren't selected.
