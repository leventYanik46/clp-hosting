Premium processing lets employers pay USCIS for a guaranteed faster decision window on an H-1B petition. It doesn't improve your odds of approval. It just speeds up when you get an answer. Because the fee and adjudication window are set by USCIS and revised periodically, confirm the current figures on the USCIS website before relying on them.

## What premium processing guarantees
USCIS commits to taking an action within a defined number of business days, whether that's approval, denial, or a request for evidence, or it refunds the fee. It's a speed guarantee on the decision, not an approval guarantee.

## The 2026 timeline
The standard premium processing window for H-1B petitions is a set number of business days from when USCIS receives the request. USCIS has adjusted these windows over time, so check the current commitment for your filing type rather than assuming a past figure.

## The fee
Premium processing carries its own fee on top of the regular filing fees, and USCIS raises it periodically to keep pace with costs. Treat any specific dollar amount as time-sensitive and verify the current fee before filing.

## When it's worth it
Use it when timing matters: a start date, a status gap, or a travel need. If there's no deadline pressure, the standard timeline may be fine. The clock on premium processing only starts when USCIS accepts the request.
