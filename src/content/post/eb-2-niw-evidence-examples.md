A strong EB-2 NIW petition lives or dies on its evidence. The Dhanasar framework gives you the three questions to answer; the evidence is how you answer them convincingly. Here are the categories that consistently carry weight and how to use them.

## Documenting national importance
Show reach beyond a single employer or city: adoption of your work across institutions, alignment with national priorities, letters explaining the broader implications, and data on how many people or organizations your endeavor affects. Connect your work to a problem the country cares about.

## Proving you're well positioned
Citations, publications, patents, funding you've secured, prior successful projects, and recommendation letters from independent experts. Progress already made — pilots, adoption, revenue, results — is some of the most persuasive evidence here.

## Supporting the waiver itself
Evidence that the standard process would be impractical or counterproductive: that you're advancing the work independently, that delay carries a real cost, or that your skills are scarce. This is the prong petitioners most often under-document.

## What to avoid
Generic letters that praise you without explaining impact, evidence that only shows local benefit, and claims without independent backing. Every exhibit should map to a specific prong.
