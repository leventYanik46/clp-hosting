E-2 and EB-5 are both investment-based routes, but they're fundamentally different products. The E-2 is a renewable temporary visa with a lower entry point. The EB-5 is a path to a green card requiring a much larger investment. Choosing between them comes down to how much you can invest and whether you want permanent residence.

## Investment size
The E-2 has no fixed minimum but typically works with a smaller, business-appropriate investment. The EB-5 requires a large, set minimum investment that's substantially higher, with the exact threshold defined by statute.

## Permanence
This is the big one. The E-2 is temporary. It's renewable indefinitely while the business runs, but never permanent on its own. The EB-5 leads to a green card and, eventually, the option of citizenship.

## Treaty nationality vs open eligibility
The E-2 requires citizenship in a treaty country. The EB-5 is open to any nationality, which makes it the only investor route for nationals of non-treaty countries.

## Timeline and choosing
The E-2 is generally faster to obtain. The EB-5 takes longer and, for some nationalities, involves visa backlogs. If you want permanent residence and can meet the higher investment, EB-5 is the fit. If you want to run a U.S. business with less capital and don't need a green card immediately, E-2 makes more sense, and some investors start with E-2 and pursue permanent residence later by another route.
